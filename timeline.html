  • emoji_people

    Southwestern Undergraduate Mathematics Research Conference, New Mexico

    As I had done a wide range of research for the department I was asked if I would like to represent the university in New Mexico at the Undergraduate mathematics Research Conference. The university sent five of us to give presentations and attend lectures from other universities. While the lectures were a hit, the highlight of the conference was the mathematics contest. The contest was a rush of brain power and adrenaline colliding to form a frantic group-solving session. With no prep and just the pens in our pockets, my team was able to finish before all the other teams. Well frankly, quite a bit ahead of the other teams which made it quite worrisome - had we missed problems or solved them incorrectly? Sweet relief came the following day we found out that we not only finished first, but got every question correct! This caused quite the controversy as we were the new university, beat the reigning champions of 5 years, and no other team came close.

    Van der Waerden Numbers

    With a building passion for computational maths I started looking for different topics which may interest me. As patterns had always caught my interest, the thought of constructing sequences with no arithmetic sequences caught my attention. During this project I learned a lot about programming and eventually made a SAT Solver to traverse my tree of possible solutions. While I didn't make any major break-throughs during this project, I was able to verify some of the previous records!

    Math Club, President

    After helping out CSU's Math Club for a few semesters I decided to step up and take a leadership position. As the club's membership was dwindling, I made it a focus to reinvent the club's image and audience. This wasn't going to be a club with monthly lectures and poor retention. While president I raised participation from 5 people a month to 30 people a week, replaced lectures with interactive projects and activities, funded 25 students to attend national mathematics conferences, hosted 3 large department parties, and changed the image of the CSU mathematics department.
